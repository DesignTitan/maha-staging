EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — All Copy Changes

CRITICAL ISSUES (Must Fix)

  1. "105 Billion" → "$10B+" — Inflated by 10x. Destroys credibility with exactly the audience this targets.
  2. "29 industries" → "9 industries" — Same problem. Pick a defensible number.
  3. Lorem ipsum in DAMN section — Placeholder text shipped to the live staging site.
  4. "Moda Metropolis / Haute Harbor / Eleganza Heights" — AI-generated placeholder in the Editorial/About section.
  5. Leadership skill = copy-paste of AI skill — Identical description and tags. Obvious error.
  6. "Director of Design" for all 6 roles — Same title at Apple, Snapchat, and Disney. Placeholder.
  7. Brand list duplicates — QVC x6, Sprint x5, Bonvoy x2, Rolex x2, Freedom Square x2.
  8. "Volkswagon" misspelled — Should be "Volkswagen." Embarrassing on a design portfolio.

HIGH-VALUE CHANGES (Brand Impact)

  1. Hero headline "REMEMBER THE NAME" → "DESIGN IS THE WEAPON" — Shift from demanding respect to demonstrating value.
  2. Floating cards — Replace vague "Making/Breaking/Defining" with real case study teasers (Marriott 1.5→4.9, Carnival +400%).
  3. Editorial section — Needs real bio and philosophy copy. The manifesto from the other site version is strong; use it.
  4. DAMN section — Replace lorem ipsum with actual proof points that earn the "DAMN." reaction.
  5. Footer CTAs — Convert from identical placeholders to real contact channels (email, LinkedIn, resume, social).
  6. All "portfolio" nav labels — Replace ~30 instances of the word "portfolio" used as placeholder with contextual metadata.
  7. "Facebook" → "Meta" — Outdated brand name.

WHAT WORKS WELL (Keep)

  • Manifesto statements: "i build fortune 500 brands / i transform industries / i scale design organizations"
  • Awards section — real award names, strong visual treatment
  • Brand wall — strong top-line client list (after spelling fixes)
  • Skills section structure — 8 disciplines with descriptions is effective (after Leadership fix)
  • Tagline: "move culture and move revenue — simultaneously"
  • "DAMN." as an editorial beat — great concept once the surrounding copy is real

SECTION REORDER SUGGESTION

Current order tells the story backwards. Consider this narrative flow:

  1. Hero (hook: who + what + why care)
  2. Stats (proof: the numbers)
  3. Brand Wall (social proof: who trusts me)
  4. Experience (career trajectory with real titles)
  5. Skills (what I bring)
  6. Awards (external validation)
  7. Editorial / About (the philosophy — "design as P&L")
  8. DAMN. (mic drop: proof points → reaction)
  9. Footer / Contact (convert: hire me, email, resume)

Drop: Industries word cloud (redundant with brand wall), Cities (lacks context unless verified). Fold their content into other sections.
